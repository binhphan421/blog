It has never been easy for me to move on. The general world paints the step as an eventual guarantee with time. And there is some truth in that, as the ghosts I once faced every waking second no longer debilitates my soul. Yet there is an instinct in me, that grips desperately onto the miseries, refusing to cleanse the very thoughts that once brought me to my knees. 

A sliver of my mind still hold on to the fading warmth of my mother. To the out of reach heart of the angel that belongs to another. To the regretful decisions of the past I can no longer recover. There is a fear, that in the loosening of my fingers, I belittle their stake in my heart. If I let go now, why did I ever cling to them at all?

Simply the musings of a child. An odd thirty-three years has done little to prevent me from melodramatic scribbles on another Sunday morning.

But this is me. I have lived. I have dreamed. I have loved. And I'll continue to do so, in the meandering, foolish and only way I know how, for as long as the world will have me. 

One day, I will move on. With the loving legacy that my mother left in my heart. With the unrelenting hope that one day, I find a reason to mention the moon's beauty. With the steadfast assurance that one day, close to my final hour, I'll look back on the sometimes terrorizing past, and smile, knowing it was mine. 

Happy Belated Birthday, punk. 

> When my birthday came around last month, I couldn't put to words the cycles and circles I was living in. And it showed in the stagnant pen.   
> Yet, as cowardly and blind as I am, as the grains of time fall, even the fool will find his way.
