When was the last time my ink dripped truth?

This is not a story of before and after. With and without. My tale is an eternal dilemma of the tail eating snake. The ego pens passages only for the heart to say this is not the way. This is not the truth.

The more I feign my triumph, my victory over grief, the more the facade is made apparent. It only takes a moment. A thought. And I am once again imprisoned, ignorant of when and where. Dreaming of anywhere, but here. 

Is this a case of confidence? A feeling I can place on to others, but rarely in myself. Is this imbalance the reason I cannot scale the wall before me? Why I am not a man?

I sometimes find myself in prayer at the sky. If only I could reach for my father's calloused hand. The answer is bound to be in the layers and layers of roughed skin I once felt when clutching his palm. How to bear the weight of their legacy. How to be the hero he was once to me.

In my delusions for the lives that would never be mine, is the concealed misery I fear to face. I once forgot that day. The look in your eyes. The coarseness of your hands. But then her day came, and suddenly I cannot forget either. I held both your hands, when the final breath left. 

And sometimes, I wish I was still there, gripping, and holding on to my dears' lives. What remains, are these barrels of guilt, locked in the vault of my heart. You both are no longer here, and it is all my fault.

The forced catharticism through this ritual of words have been a farce. I am not honest. To the world I strike onto paper. To those who try to understand. To myself. 

I never saw the conquest I wrote of. To this day, since 15 years ago, since 8 months ago, I am still right by their side, clutching onto the end of our stories. 

Why do I desperately seek to prove that I am happy? Or feel that failure in being so, is shameful? As if, a beautiful ending is a guarantee I missed. If omnipresent, all-powerful time can heal all wounds, why am I here, drenched in my own woe?



Why can't I see that is okay? To grieve. To hurt. To wallow. Why do I not trust in my own humanity? I am only human, am I not?

Suffering is the memorial of the same coin. For the day it flips, and I head to long awaited bliss, is also the day, I stand on the foundation made by my struggles. 

Do not look away. Do not forget. You are here. For today, until tomorrow, that is enough.
