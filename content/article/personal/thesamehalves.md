How strange it was, not seeing your grin in front of me, as I ordered at our com tam restaurant. 

Not asking you what you want to order. Not cleaning your fork and spoon. Not protesting when you insist I finish your plate. 

The thought of you didn't freeze my heart. A sign of recovery, I guess. 

But there is still a void shaped like you wherever I look, covering the present from my sight. I would fear love for my remainder, knowing the blindness loss has inflicted, inflicts and will inflict.

---

Yet, in plain sight, my desires reflect in the pit of my eyes: I want to love, and be loved. But you are gone, and the devotion I once held now bloats me with uncertainties. 

So I pray it finds me. To make a new life of me, so I no longer mud in my own misery.  

---

Hah! I am still the fool you know. Wanting to be someone's someone, having already surrendered. 

In that world, my fancies will only become obsessions, my loneliness distorting their realities. There, I did not fall for a person. I tripped on my imagination. 

In love, we dance in pairs. Two distinct halves of the same story. How easy I forget, when clutching onto mine.
